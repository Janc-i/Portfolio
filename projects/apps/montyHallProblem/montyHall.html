Monty Hall Problem Simulation

The Monty Hall problem is a probability puzzle based on a game show. The contestant is given three doors, behind one of which is a prize, while the other two doors hide nothing. The contestant chooses a door, but before it is opened, the host, who knows what is behind each door, opens one of the other two doors, revealing that there is nothing behind it. The contestant is then given the option to switch their choice to the other unopened door or stick with their original choice. The question is: Should the contestant switch their choice to the other door to increase their chances of winning the prize?
